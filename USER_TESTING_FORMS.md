## Form 5: Task Scripts for Participants

**Form Title:** Student Marketplace - Testing Tasks

**Form Description:**
Please complete the following tasks while thinking aloud. Describe what you're doing and any thoughts or confusion as you go.

---

### Task 1: Account Creation & Profile Setup (Target: 3 minutes)

**Scenario:** You are a new student who wants to start buying and selling items on the Student Marketplace.

**Your Tasks:**
1. Create a new account using your email address
2. Complete your profile by adding your first name and last name
3. Optionally upload a profile picture

**Success Criteria:**
- Account is created and you can log in
- Profile shows your name
- You can navigate to your profile page

---

### Task 2: Create a Listing with AI Price Prediction (Target: 5 minutes)

**Scenario:** You have a textbook you no longer need and want to sell it on the marketplace.

**Your Tasks:**
1. Navigate to the listing creation page
2. Create a listing for a textbook (use ISBN: 9780134685991 or any valid ISBN)
3. Use the AI price prediction feature to get a suggested price
4. Complete the listing with all required information
5. Upload at least one image
6. Submit the listing

**Success Criteria:**
- AI price prediction was obtained
- Listing appears on the marketplace
- All fields are filled correctly

---

### Task 3: Search and Filter Items (Target: 2 minutes)

**Scenario:** You're looking for electronics items under £50.

**Your Tasks:**
1. Search for "electronics" or browse the Electronics category
2. Apply a price filter to show only items under £50
3. Find at least one item that matches your criteria

**Success Criteria:**
- Search results are displayed
- Price filter is applied correctly
- Matching items are found

---

### Task 4: Contact a Seller (Target: 1 minute)

**Scenario:** You've found an item you're interested in and want to ask the seller a question.

**Your Tasks:**
1. Select a product listing
2. Find the option to contact the seller
3. Send a message asking about the item's condition

**Success Criteria:**
- Message is sent successfully
- Conversation appears in your messages

---

### Task 5: View and Edit Your Listing (Target: 2 minutes)

**Scenario:** You want to update the price of the listing you created earlier.

**Your Tasks:**
1. Navigate to your listings/profile
2. Find the listing you created
3. Edit the listing to change the price
4. Save the changes

**Success Criteria:**
- Listing is found in your profile
- Price is updated successfully
- Changes are saved

## Appendix A: SUS Score Calculation Guide

### Calculating SUS Score:

1. For odd-numbered questions (1, 3, 5, 7, 9): Subtract 1 from the score
2. For even-numbered questions (2, 4, 6, 8, 10): Subtract the score from 5
3. Sum all converted scores
4. Multiply by 2.5 to get a score out of 100

**Target Score: ≥70/100** (Good usability)

### Score Interpretation:
- 0-25: Worst imaginable
- 26-39: Poor
- 40-52: OK
- 53-73: Good
- 74-85: Excellent
- 86-100: Best imaginable

## Appendix B: Data Analysis Template

### Quantitative Metrics to Calculate:

1. **Task Completion Times**
   - Mean completion time per task
   - Median completion time per task
   - Standard deviation per task
   - % within target time

2. **Success Rates**
   - Task success rate (binary: complete/incomplete)
   - Assisted vs. unassisted completion rate

3. **Error Metrics**
   - Mean errors per task
   - Error types frequency

4. **SUS Score**
   - Individual scores
   - Mean score
   - Standard deviation

5. **AI Acceptance Rate**
   - % who used AI pricing
   - % who accepted suggested price
   - % who modified price
   - % who rejected suggestion

### Qualitative Analysis Process:

1. **Transcription:** Convert think-aloud recordings to text
2. **Open Coding:** Identify initial themes from comments
3. **Axial Coding:** Group related codes into categories
4. **Selective Coding:** Identify core themes
5. **Affinity Diagramming:** Cluster similar comments
6. **Nielsen Mapping:** Map issues to usability heuristics
7. **Insight Generation:** Create actionable recommendations

## Appendix D: Ethical Considerations

1. **Informed Consent:** All participants must sign consent before participating
2. **Data Anonymization:** Use participant IDs (P01, P02, etc.) instead of names
3. **Right to Withdraw:** Participants can stop at any time without consequence
4. **Data Storage:** Secure storage of all recordings and forms
5. **Confidentiality:** Results reported in aggregate only
6. **Debrief:** Explain study purpose after completion
